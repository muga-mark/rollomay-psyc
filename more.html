Rolly May was an American Psychologist who is often referred to as the father of Existential Psychology. He was an in- fluential American Psychologist who helped established a new branch of psychology.

He came to this approach through personal hardship. As explained, Existentialism deals on man's search for meaing and purpose in life. May was stricken by tuberculosis, a bacterial lung infection, and was hospitalized for several years. During this illness, May began to explore the meaning of life in face of death. This curiosity eventually led May to study clinical psychology, and was the recipient of the first PhD in clinical psychology by Columbia University.

May believed that people may lack the courage to face their destiny and while in the process of fleeing from it, they give up much their freedom. In contrast, healthy people challenge their destiny, cherish their freedom, and live authentically with other people and themselves. They recognize the inevitability of death and have the courage to live life in the present.
